First songs in Geometry Dash

Published on February 18, 2020

On November 9, 2014, what is possibly the post important update of all time was released: 1.9. RobTop considers this, alongside 2.2 to be the among the most important updates of all time.

1.9 was undoubtedly the biggest update at the time. It added 2 new levels, Blast Processing and Theory of Everything 2, something that hadn't been done since 1.6, when Electroman Adventures and Clubstep, the first official demon level, were added to the game. Appropriately, 2 more songs were added to the game for use in the level editor. A variety of new blocks were added to the game, increasing the amount of variety that levels could have, and color channels were implemented allowing objects that had a color property to use 1 out of 4 colors. The amount of colors would become infinite in 2.0, and 999 in 2.1. A new form called the wave was implemented, which was the first form to be added to the game since 1.5, when the UFO was added.

But that's just the start. By far the most important feature to ever come to the game was implemented in this version. The feature? Custom songs.

RobTop allowing users to use songs from Newgrounds revolutionized Geometry Dash forever. Players were no longer restricted to the same 18 songs, as there were over 500,000 songs uploaded to Newgrounds at the time. Geometry Dash may have been the reason for so many artist's spike in popularity, such as Hinkik and Creo. Unfortunately, some users abused this new system to upload copyrighted music so that they could use it in Geometry Dash. In response, Newgrounds and RobTop decided that users would need to be scouted in order to have their music used in GD, but this wasn't enough, so a custom whitelist was implemented so that RobTop would need to manually approve NG accounts.

What were the first custom songs used?

The problem with some users is that they'd change the song in their level to a custom song (MaJackO was notorious for this), which would ruin the sync of the song. Here are some of the first NG songs used in GD:

  • Endgame by Waterflame (587069), used in Endgame by vGhost01 (2814868)
  • Stereo Madness 2 by ForeverBound (590577), used in Stereo Madness 2 by iIElDanteIi (2816038)
  • Chaoz Impact by ParagonX9 (152047), used in Spectex Impact by Spectex (2827718)
  • On Fire by dj-Nate (65332), used in Bright Flame by Headwind (2828436)

And many, many, MANY more! The ID count is nearing 1 million, but RobTop already has that covered for when 2.2 comes. Let's hope that 2.2 comes before 1 million song ID's are taken up.
